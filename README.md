# Applied AI Engineering
### Приложно ИИ инженерство · Sofia

> A practical, industry-led course that bridges the gap between academia and the AI industry in Sofia. Every lecture is prepared and delivered by an engineer who builds this stuff for a living.

---

## Why this course, why now

AI — and LLMs in particular — is reshaping software engineering and creating a new role: the **AI Engineer**. The technology moves faster than any curriculum can keep up with, unless you work in it daily. This course closes that gap by bringing practicing engineers from Sofia's AI industry (and beyond) to teach the topic they ship every day.

It's a deliberate bridge between academia and industry: real practices, real systems, real war stories — not slides about theory that's already a year out of date.

## Who it's for

BSc/MSc students and early-career engineers in AI, Software Engineering, or Computer Science who want to either:

- **Build deep technical skill** — become an AI engineer who can design and ship production-grade systems, **or**
- **Build product & business sense** — turn AI into something people actually use, with real users, without living in the internals.

You don't have to pick a lane on day one. The course is designed to support both kinds of builder.

## How we teach

Practical over theoretical. Each session gives you the **key concepts and the real-world context**, then leans on *your* questions to surface what matters for *your* project. You form a team early, pick something to build, and work on it across the whole course. The finale is a **Demo Day** — a working product and a pitch, not a wall of text.

- One industry expert per topic, teaching what they actually work on
- Ask questions — we optimize the room for what's relevant to what you're building

## Curriculum

A first-draft outline lives in [`/curriculum`](./curriculum). It's a **starting point** — the final topics and their order will shift based on community feedback and which experts step up to lead each session.

Four phases:

1. **Intro** — why AI, the platform shift, the opportunity to build
2. **Capabilities of AI** — text, image, video, voice, and how to evaluate non-deterministic systems
3. **Building AI** — from 2024-era RAG → 2025 workflows → 2026–27 autonomous agents, plus product, tooling, and security
4. **Final** — Demo Day

## Grading

- **Test 1** — midterm, checks your AI intuition and the capabilities half of the course
- **Group Project** — build an application using what you've learned; culminates in a Demo Day pitch. The bar: *make something people want.*
- **Test 2** — final exam covering the building half of the course

## The group project & Demo Day

Teams of 3–5. Pick a project early, build it throughout the course, and present it on **Demo Day**: a working demo plus a pitch that convinces the room of both the value of the project and the expertise behind it.

Learning to communicate, delegate, and ship as a team is part of the point — it's the skill the industry actually hires for.
