# Curriculum — Candidate Topics

> ⚠️ **This is a first draft and not a fixed running order.** The items below are *candidate lectures*, grouped by type. Any of them may be **merged, split, reordered, dropped, or added** depending on which industry experts step up and what the community tells us matters. The actual sequence is set closer to the start. Suggestions welcome — see [*Get involved*](../README.md#get-involved).

Each lecture runs 2h lecture + 2h practical lab. We're aiming for ~15 sessions across the course.

## Types

| Type | What it's about |
|------|-----------------|
| **Foundations** | Intuition for how AI actually works |
| **Capabilities** | What AI can do, across modalities |
| **Building** | Engineering production systems — data, tooling, frameworks, agents, security |
| **Product** | What to build, who for, and how to make it make sense |

## Candidate lectures

| Topic | Type | What it covers |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Course Intro** | Foundations | Who is this for, why now, the AI platform shift — ideally opening with a *live demo* of an AI agent doing a real task, then unpacking how it works. |
| **Machine Learning & AI intuition** | Foundations | A general feel for what AI is and how it's trained — without turning into an ML theory course. |
| **AI System Architecture** | Foundations | Just enough on training, pre-training, and attention to make good engineering decisions (model choice, fine-tuning, context) — without becoming a deep-learning course. |
| **Text models & LLMs** | Capabilities | Using and integrating LLMs in depth, including model selection: open vs. closed, cost vs. performance, fine-tune vs. prompt, small vs. large, distillation. Parameters, structured output, comparing models. |
| **Image & Video models** | Capabilities | Multimodal AI — image classification, image and video generation. |
| **Voice & Audio models** | Capabilities | Multimodal AI — STT, TTS, voice agents, real-time voice (Kyutai), voice clones and safety. |
| **Quality & Evaluations** | Capabilities | Evaluating non-deterministic systems in practice — evals, the Data Engine, "chasing the 9s," eval frameworks. |
| **Local models** | Building | Running open models locally and on your own data; when local makes sense vs. API (cost, privacy, control); fine-tuning and LoRA. |
| **Data integrations (2024: RAG)** | Building | Integrating AI with data systems — DBs, APIs, unstructured data, vector DBs, RAG, MLOps & production pipelines. |
| **Data Engine & Data Modelling** | Building | Data modelling as the core ML task: chunking strategies, embeddings vs. hybrid search, freshness & versioning, feedback loops, synthetic data, and gold evaluation sets. |
| **AI Tooling — Software Engineering with Agents** | Building | Using AI to build faster — the mindset shift, the V-cycle, coding and shipping more with AI. |
| **Frameworks & AI Workflows (2025)** | Building | Designing complex workflows — frameworks vs. custom code, deterministic workflows vs. autonomous agents. |
| **Agentic systems (2026–27)** | Building | Designing autonomous agents and where their capabilities are heading — "superagents," multi-agent loops, autonomy and tool-use. |
| **Security & Safety** | Building | What security means for AI systems; industry perspectives (e.g. Martin Fowler's POV). |
| **Product Design & MVP** | Product | What to build, how to design it, how to test the market and pick a project — prototyping, designing for non-determinism, human-in-the-loop, AI-native UI vs. wrappers. |
| **Pitch & Business** | Product | How to pitch the product and make it make economic sense — unit economics (token costs), scaling, pitch prep, what VCs look for. |
| **Demo Day** | Product | Final project presentation, live demo, and judging. |
